Privacy Policy

Effective July 16, 2026

The short version: Horas collects nothing. There are no servers, no analytics, no ads, and no tracking of any kind. Your data stays on your devices and, only if you turn it on, in your personal iCloud.

What data the app handles

Horas displays your Dreaming Spanish progress: input minutes, hours totals, streak, and daily goal. To do this it stores on your device:

  • Your Dreaming Spanish login session, kept in the iOS keychain/app storage on your device only.
  • Your progress data (hours, minutes, streak, goal), stored locally so the app and its widgets can read it.

Where your data goes

  • To Dreaming Spanish: your credentials and progress requests go directly from your device to dreamingspanish.com, exactly as if you used their website. We never see them.
  • To your iCloud (optional): if you enable iCloud sync, your progress data is stored in your personal iCloud key-value storage so your other devices can read it. Apple's privacy terms govern that storage; we have no access to it.
  • To your Apple Watch: progress data moves between your iPhone and your paired watch over Apple's local device-to-device channel.
  • Nowhere else. Horas has no backend, no third-party SDKs, no analytics, and no crash reporting. Nothing is collected, sold, or shared.

What we can see

Nothing. We operate no servers, so there is nothing for us to receive, store, or lose.

Data deletion

Delete the app and all locally stored data goes with it. If you enabled iCloud sync, you can additionally clear the app's iCloud data from Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage, or simply toggle sync off and delete the app.

Children

Horas does not knowingly handle data from children and collects no data from anyone.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if a future version adds opt-in crash reporting), the change will be listed here with a new effective date before it ships.
